Mariano Rivera

The Greatest Closer

Mariano Rivera is a Panamanian baseball player, currently retired, that played his entire professional career with the New York Yankees from 1995 to 2013. During this time he accrued more saves than any other pitcher in history (652), was voted to the AL All-star team 13 times, and won 5 World Series championships. The right hander was known not only as a dominant closer, but as a unique pitcher due to the fact that unlike most pitchers he didn't have 3-5 pitches that he alternated - he instead threw only one pitch, his "cutter," a fastball with a very late break, and with pinpoint accuracy he was able to put up these impressive career numbers while keeping a 2.21 ERA and a 1.00 WHIP. Due to Mariano's dominance for nearly two decades with the same club, the Yankees organization decided in 2014 to retire his number (42) out of respect for the former closer.

Mariano Rivera Timeline

  • 1969: Mariano is born in Panama
  • 1985: 16 year old Mariano survives the sinking of his family's fishing boat
  • 1988: Mariano first joins an amateur Panamanian team as a shortstop
  • 1990: Yankees scout Herb Raybourn signs Mariano to a minor-league contract as a pitcher
  • 1995: Rivera makes his MLB debut as a starter and struggles tremendously
  • 1996: Yankees shift Mariano to the setup position, where he excels
  • 1997: Yankees opt out of option on closer John Wetteland, decide to make Mariano their starting closer
  • 1999: "Enter Sandman" by Metallica first gets played upon Mariano's exit from the bullpen, a song that would stick to this routine for the rest of his career
  • 2002: Rivera records his 225th save, passing Dave Righetti as the Yankees all times saves leader
  • 2009: Rivera closes out his 5th and final World Series Championship against the Philadelphia Phillies
  • 2013: Mariano pitches his final game
